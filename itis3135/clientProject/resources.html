DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations) ▼

FL Studio21

I mainly use Fl Studio when it comes to making beats. It is a program for entry-level producers, but its power should not be underestimated today. Most mainstream trap beats are made using this software, and the possibilites are truly endless when you learn it.

Ableton

Ableton is a DAW that I don't have as much experience in, but it's power cannot be underestimated. I find Ableton to be especially useful when I am wokring with genres outsite of trap, as it allows for a lot more experimentation. It is not as user-friendly as FL Studio, but it is a great tool for those who are willing to learn it.

VST Plugins ▼

HalfTime

HalfTime is a plugin that I use daily, and it helps me create melodies when I am lacking inspiration. It can be put on any mixer track, and it will half the tempo of the audio for that track. I especially like this plugin becuase you are able to cut certain frequencies from being processed, which allows for a lot of those muddier, lower frequences to be bypassed.

Xpand!2

Xpand!2 is a plugin I have only recently started incorpororating more into my workflow, but it has already become one of my favorite generator plugins. The ability to layer 4 sounds in one instance of Xpand is its best feature, and its stock sounds/effects are really all you need to make whatever genre of music you want.

Hardware ▼

Volt1 Audio Interace

At home, I use the Volt1 audio interface. It is not best interface on the market, it is also is not the most expensive. This, or the more popular FocusRite are great options for new producers, or even just as a small portable interface I can travel with.

Kontakt S61 Midi Keyboard

The Kontakt S61 Mk2 is my main midi keyboard, and it has helped me produce almost all of my beats. It has 61 keys, with built in buttons for things like recording, pausing and even changing instruments. I was lucky enough to find one for about 5$ at a GoodWill, but brand new these keyboards are not cheap.
